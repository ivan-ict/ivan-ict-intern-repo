# Learning from User Onboarding Calls

**Milestone:** Gathering Customer Insights
**Task:** Learning from User Onboarding Calls (#16)

---

## Goal

The goal of this task is to listen to recorded onboarding calls and extract insights about user expectations, struggles, and first impressions of Focus Bear. This helps identify onboarding friction points and improve the first-time user experience.

---

## Why is this important?

Onboarding calls are valuable because they show real user behaviour, expectations, and confusion when using the product for the first time. Product Managers can learn where users struggle, what they expect from the product, and what features are most important to them. This helps improve onboarding design and user retention.

---

## Research & Learn

### Why are onboarding calls valuable for product teams?

Onboarding calls are valuable because:

- They show real first-time user experience.
- Teams can observe where users get confused.
- Teams can understand user expectations and goals.
- They help identify onboarding friction points.
- They help improve user activation and retention.

### What should PMs look for when analysing new user feedback?

PMs should look for:

- First impressions of the product.
- Where users get confused.
- What users expect the product to do.
- What features users are most interested in.
- Any technical problems during setup.
- How long onboarding takes.
- Whether users successfully complete onboarding.

### What are common reasons users drop off during onboarding?

Common reasons include:

- Onboarding takes too long.
- Instructions are unclear.
- Too many steps in setup.
- Technical issues during installation.
- Users do not understand the value of the product.
- The setup process feels complicated.

---

## Insights from Onboarding Calls

After listening to onboarding calls, several common themes were identified.

### 1. First Impressions

- Users think the idea of Focus Bear is interesting and useful.
- Some users feel the onboarding process is a bit long.
- Some users are confused at the beginning about what to do first.
- Users like the idea of blocking distractions and building habits.

### 2. Pain Points

- Some users had difficulty installing or setting up the app.
- Some users did not understand how to start a focus session.
- Some users were confused by too many onboarding steps.
- Some users were unsure how to customize blocking settings.
- Some technical issues occurred during setup.

### 3. User Goals

Most users want to use Focus Bear to:

- Reduce distractions.
- Improve productivity.
- Build better habits.
- Manage study/work time.
- Avoid social media during work/study.

---

## Suggested Small Improvement

One small improvement that could make onboarding smoother is to create a **simple step-by-step onboarding checklist**. This checklist would guide users through installation, setting up blocking, creating their first focus session, and starting their first session. This would reduce confusion and help users complete onboarding faster.

---

## Reflection

### What was the user’s biggest struggle during onboarding?

The biggest struggle was understanding the onboarding steps and setting up the blocking features correctly. Some users were confused about what to do next during the setup process.

### What was one thing the user liked about Focus Bear?

Users liked the idea of blocking distracting websites and apps to help them focus. They also liked the habit-building feature and the concept of structured focus sessions.

### What’s one improvement you’d suggest based on the call?

I would suggest simplifying the onboarding process and adding a clear step-by-step guide or checklist so users know exactly what to do during setup.

---

## Summary

From the onboarding calls, the main issues were onboarding complexity, setup confusion, and technical setup difficulties. However, users liked the main idea of the product and its focus and habit-building features. Improving the onboarding experience would likely improve user activation and retention.
