## Professionalism at Focus Bear – Reflection

### Why Professionalism Matters

Professionalism at Focus Bear is about maintaining a respectful and collaborative environment while still enjoying a relaxed and supportive culture. Even though the company encourages a fun and informal atmosphere, team members are still expected to communicate clearly, take responsibility for their work, and treat others with respect.

For a Product Manager intern, professionalism is especially important because the role often involves coordinating with designers, developers, and stakeholders. Clear communication and respectful collaboration help ensure that ideas are understood and projects move forward efficiently.

---

## Professional vs. Unprofessional Behaviour in a Casual Work Environment

### Professional Behaviour

Examples of professional behaviour include:

- Communicating clearly and respectfully in messages and meetings
- Being prepared for meetings and respecting other people’s time
- Listening carefully to teammates and considering their perspectives
- Taking responsibility for tasks and following through on commitments
- Being open to feedback and willing to improve
- Maintaining a positive and supportive attitude toward teammates

### Unprofessional Behaviour

Examples of behaviour that can harm the work environment include:

- Ignoring messages or failing to respond when collaboration is needed
- Interrupting others or dismissing their ideas
- Making disrespectful or offensive comments
- Showing up to meetings unprepared
- Ignoring feedback or becoming defensive when receiving suggestions

Even in a relaxed culture, these behaviours can negatively affect team productivity and trust.

---

## Respectful Communication in Remote Teams

Because many teams work remotely, communication style becomes very important.

Respectful communication in remote teams includes:

- Using a polite and professional tone in written messages
- Avoiding assumptions and asking clarifying questions when something is unclear
- Being mindful that text messages can sometimes be misinterpreted
- Giving teammates time to respond and respecting their working hours
- Acknowledging others’ contributions and showing appreciation for their work

In remote environments, clarity and empathy help maintain strong teamwork.

---

## Giving and Receiving Feedback Professionally

### Giving Feedback

When giving feedback, it is important to focus on the work rather than the person. Some good practices include:

- Explaining the issue clearly
- Suggesting possible improvements
- Providing feedback respectfully and constructively

Example:

> “I think the feature flow could be clearer if we simplify the onboarding steps.”

### Receiving Feedback

When receiving feedback:

- Listen carefully before responding
- Assume the feedback is meant to help improve the work
- Ask clarifying questions if necessary
- Use the feedback to improve future work

For a Product Manager intern, feedback is valuable because it helps develop better decision-making and communication skills.

## Experience with Effective Teamwork

I have experienced effective teamwork in previous academic and professional projects where team members communicated openly and supported each other.

The key factors that made the teamwork successful included:

- Clear division of responsibilities
- Regular communication and updates
- Respect for each person’s expertise
- Constructive discussions when solving problems

This type of teamwork creates an environment where everyone feels comfortable contributing ideas.

---

## Ensuring Clear, Respectful, and Inclusive Communication

To maintain professional communication, I will:

- Write clear and structured messages
- Avoid ambiguous or emotional wording in written communication
- Listen actively during meetings
- Encourage input from other team members
- Respect different perspectives and backgrounds

These practices help build trust and improve collaboration across the team.

---

## Creating a Positive and Professional Work Environment

A positive work environment can be created by:

- Supporting teammates when they face challenges
- Sharing knowledge and helping others when possible
- Maintaining a friendly and respectful attitude
- Celebrating team progress and achievements

Even small actions such as acknowledging someone’s effort can strengthen team morale.

---

## Preparing for Meetings with Supervisors and Colleagues

To prepare effectively for meetings, I will:

- Review the project brief and current progress before the meeting
- Prepare questions or discussion points in advance
- Bring updates on completed tasks and current blockers
- Take notes during meetings to track action items

Being prepared helps meetings stay efficient and productive.

---

## Being Proactive During the Internship

Being proactive means taking initiative rather than waiting for instructions.

Examples include:

- Researching solutions before asking questions
- Proposing ideas based on research or analysis
- Providing regular updates on progress
- Identifying potential issues early

As a Product Manager intern, proactive behaviour demonstrates ownership and helps the team move faster.

---

## Following Up and Escalating Issues

If I need information or action from a colleague or supervisor, I will follow a structured communication approach.

1. Start with email for general communication
2. Use team chat tools (such as Discord or Teams) for quicker responses
3. Send an SMS message if something becomes urgent
4. Call directly only in true emergencies

If I do not receive a response after a reasonable time, I will send a polite follow-up message. Escalation should only happen when necessary and should always remain respectful.

---

## What I Will Avoid

To maintain professionalism, there are several behaviours I will avoid:

- Waiting passively for instructions instead of taking initiative
- Attending meetings without preparation
- Ignoring feedback or repeating the same mistakes
- Asking questions that could easily be answered through basic research
- Failing to communicate progress or blockers
- Relying on only one communication channel for urgent issues

Avoiding these habits will help maintain smooth collaboration with the team.

---

## Key Practice I Will Follow

One professional habit I will adopt during my internship is providing regular updates on my work progress.

At the end of each working day, I will send a brief update summarizing:

- What tasks I completed
- What I am currently working on
- Any blockers or questions

This helps ensure transparency and allows supervisors to provide guidance quickly if needed.

---

## Escalation of Major Professional Issues

If I observe a serious breach of professional conduct or behaviour that could harm the team environment, I understand that it may be necessary to escalate the issue appropriately.

According to the onboarding guidance, the CEO’s contact details are available for reporting major concerns if required.

However, escalation should only be used responsibly and after considering appropriate communication channels.

---

## Key Learning

Professionalism at Focus Bear is not about formality or strict rules. Instead, it focuses on respect, accountability, clear communication, and collaboration.

By practicing proactive communication, preparing for meetings, and responding constructively to feedback, I can contribute positively to the team while also developing my skills as a Product Manager.
