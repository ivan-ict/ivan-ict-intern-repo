# OHS -- Laptop Ergonomics Reflection

## 1. Current Workspace Setup

I currently use an ergonomic desk setup rather than working directly on
my laptop. My main device is a MacBook Pro M2 connected via USB-C to a
Lenovo 27" 4K USB-C monitor (N27p) with 65W power delivery. The monitor
is mounted on an adjustable monitor arm, allowing precise control of
height and viewing angle to match my eye level.

I use an external mechanical keyboard (Leopold FC660MBT Coral with
Cherry MX Silent Red switches) and a Logitech M650 Silent mouse. I
intentionally selected silent devices because I prefer a quiet work
environment that supports deep focus.

My desk and chair are from IKEA. I also use a desk lamp to improve
lighting during evening work sessions. Occasionally, I play soft
background music through a JBL Bluetooth speaker to create a calm and
productive atmosphere.

## 2. Risks of Using a Laptop Without Proper Ergonomics

Using a laptop without external equipment can lead to several health
risks:

- Neck strain from looking down at the screen
- Rounded shoulders and upper back tension
- Wrist strain due to improper keyboard height
- Lower back discomfort from poor sitting posture
- Eye strain from small screens and improper viewing distance

Over time, these issues may result in chronic pain and reduced
productivity.

## 3. Ergonomic Equipment That Improves Posture

The following equipment helps improve posture and reduce strain:

- External monitor
- Adjustable monitor arm
- Laptop stand
- External keyboard and mouse
- Ergonomic chair
- Proper desk lighting

My current setup already includes most of these elements, which
significantly reduces physical strain during long work sessions.

## 4. Workspace Adjustments Based on Best Practices

I adjusted my workspace according to ergonomic principles:

- The top of the monitor is aligned at approximately eye level
- The monitor is positioned at arm's length distance
- Shoulders remain relaxed while typing
- Elbows are at approximately 90 degrees
- Wrists stay in a neutral position
- My back is supported by the chair
- Feet rest flat on the floor

The adjustable monitor arm allows easy fine-tuning whenever needed.

## 5. Behavioural Habits to Reduce Strain

In addition to equipment, daily habits are essential. I regularly:

- Stand up and move after long focused sessions
- Do light housework as active breaks
- Stretch my neck and shoulders
- Consciously check and correct posture
- Rest my eyes by looking away from the screen

These habits have been part of my routine for several years while
working freelance, remote, and hybrid roles in Vietnam.

## 6. Equipment Improvements Identified

Although my setup is already ergonomic, I identified one possible
improvement: adding a small footrest to further optimise lower body
posture during extended sitting sessions.

I also plan to maintain balanced lighting conditions to prevent eye
fatigue, especially during late evening work.

## 7. Behavioural Changes Implemented for This Task

For this task, I focused on using structured movement reminders
throughout a full workday. I scheduled reminders to:

- Stand up every 45--60 minutes
- Stretch shoulders and neck
- Perform short eye-rest exercises

This helped reinforce habits that I previously practiced more
intuitively.

## 8. Results After One Full Workday

After applying structured reminders for a full workday, I noticed:

- Reduced neck stiffness
- Better sustained concentration
- Less lower back fatigue
- Increased awareness of posture alignment

Even though I have maintained ergonomic habits for years, structured
reminders improved consistency and awareness.

## 9. Long-Term Commitment to OHS

Having worked in freelance, remote, and hybrid environments for many
years, I understand that productivity is closely linked to physical
wellbeing. Maintaining an ergonomic workspace is essential for long-term
health and sustainable performance.

I will continue refining both my equipment setup and daily habits to
ensure safe and healthy desk-based work practices.
