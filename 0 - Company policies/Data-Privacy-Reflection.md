# Data Privacy & Confidentiality Reflection

## Research & Learn

### Key Takeaways from Focus Bear’s Privacy Policy

Focus Bear collects and processes personal data in a lawful, fair, and transparent way. The company follows international privacy regulations such as the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) to ensure that user data is handled responsibly. Personal data is only collected when it is necessary for specific purposes such as managing user accounts, providing services, improving the application, and communicating with users.

The policy explains that Focus Bear acts as the data controller, meaning the company is responsible for how personal data is collected, stored, and used. To maintain compliance with privacy regulations, Focus Bear works with a Data Protection Officer (DPO) and trusted third-party processors who assist with services such as authentication, cloud storage, analytics, and payment processing.

Another important point is that Focus Bear implements technical and organisational security measures to protect personal data. These measures help ensure the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information. The company also provides users with rights under GDPR, including the right to access, correct, delete, or restrict the use of their personal data.

### Types of Confidential Data at Focus Bear

Several types of data handled by Focus Bear can be considered confidential. Identification data such as email addresses and phone numbers are sensitive because they can be used to identify or contact users. Social and lifestyle data collected through habits or surveys may also reveal personal information about users' daily routines or preferences.

Academic and professional information provided during surveys can also be confidential because it relates to a user’s occupation or background. Financial data used for subscriptions is especially sensitive, even though it is processed through secure payment providers such as Stripe.

Technical data such as device information, operating systems, and error logs can also be sensitive because it may reveal how users interact with the application. Additionally, some special category data may be indirectly revealed through user habits, such as religious practices or health-related activities. Because of this, Focus Bear applies strong security measures such as encryption and anonymisation.

### Best Practices for Handling Confidential Data

Handling confidential data responsibly requires several important practices. One key practice is limiting access to data so that only authorised team members who need the information for their work can view it. Another important practice is using secure systems and trusted service providers to store and process information.

Data should also be anonymised or aggregated whenever possible so that individuals cannot be directly identified. Strong encryption and authentication systems should be used to protect accounts and sensitive information. It is also important to follow company policies and avoid downloading or storing confidential data on unsecured devices.

### Responding to a Suspected Data Breach

If a suspected data breach or accidental disclosure occurs, it should be reported immediately to the appropriate authority within the organisation. Focus Bear’s policies indicate that security breaches must be evaluated and managed according to GDPR requirements.

The team should investigate the incident to understand what data was affected and how the breach occurred. If required by law, the company may need to report the breach to the relevant supervisory authority. Quick reporting and transparent communication are essential to minimise harm and prevent further security issues.

## Reflection

### Steps to Handle Data Securely in Daily Tasks

To handle data securely in my daily tasks, I should always follow company privacy policies and avoid accessing information that is not necessary for my work. I should ensure that any data I work with is stored in secure systems provided by the company rather than personal devices.

Another important step is being careful when sharing information through communication tools such as email, Discord, or documentation platforms. Sensitive data should only be shared with authorised team members and through approved channels.

### Safe Storage, Sharing, and Disposal of Sensitive Information

Sensitive information should always be stored in secure systems that use encryption and access control. Files should not be saved locally unless absolutely necessary, and if they are, they should be protected and deleted once they are no longer needed.

When sharing information, it is important to confirm that the recipient is authorised to receive it. Secure platforms should be used instead of public or unsecured communication channels. When information is no longer required, it should be permanently deleted or anonymised to prevent future misuse.

### Common Mistakes That Lead to Data Privacy Issues

Many data privacy issues occur due to simple mistakes. One common mistake is sharing confidential information with the wrong person or through an insecure channel. Another mistake is storing sensitive data on personal devices without proper protection.

People may also accidentally expose data by including confidential information in screenshots, logs, or public documentation. These issues can be avoided by carefully reviewing information before sharing it and following company security guidelines at all times.
