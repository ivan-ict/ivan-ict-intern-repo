# Working with UX Designers (#7)

## Overview

This task focuses on understanding how Product Managers collaborate with UX Designers to ensure features are intuitive, accessible, and aligned with user needs.

---

## Goal

To evaluate UX designs, identify usability improvements, and understand how PMs contribute to UX decisions.

---

## Why This Is Important

Good UX design ensures:

- Features are easy to understand and use
- Users can achieve their goals efficiently
- The product drives engagement and retention

PMs help align **user needs, business goals, and design decisions**.

---

## 🔍 Research & Learn

### 1. Role of PM in UX Design

Product Managers:

- Define user problems and goals
- Provide context and requirements to designers
- Review designs to ensure alignment with product objectives
- Validate whether designs solve the intended problem

---

### 2. Ensuring Accessibility & Usability

PMs ensure:

- Clear user flows and navigation
- Minimal cognitive load
- Consistent behaviour across screens
- Accessibility (e.g., readable text, clear actions)

---

### 3. Providing Constructive UX Feedback

Best practices:

- Focus on **user outcomes**, not personal preference
- Use specific examples (e.g., confusion points)
- Suggest improvements, not just criticism
- Align feedback with product goals

---

## UX Design Reviewed

### Feature: Custom Blocking Schedule (Figma Design)

Design Link:
https://www.figma.com/design/vu5gycg6YKxt6uIH6d2cGX/Focus-Bear-App

---

## UX Evaluation

### Strengths

- Clear purpose: allows flexible blocking schedules
- Visual structure supports time-based configuration
- Supports multiple modes (strict vs gentle)

---

## Areas for Improvement

### 1. Complexity of Configuration

#### Issue

- Multiple options (time segments, modes, block lists, friction settings)
- Can overwhelm new users

#### Impact

- High cognitive load
- Users may abandon setup

---

### 2. Lack of Clear User Flow Guidance

#### Issue

- No clear step-by-step guidance for first-time users
- Users may not know:
    - Where to start
    - What to configure first

#### Impact

- Confusion during onboarding
- Reduced feature adoption

---

## Suggested Improvement

### Add Guided Setup (Onboarding Flow)

Introduce a **step-by-step setup flow**:

1. Select schedule (e.g., weekdays/weekends)
2. Choose blocking mode (strict/gentle)
3. Select apps/websites to block
4. Confirm setup

---

### Why This Improves Usability

- Reduces cognitive load
- Helps users understand feature purpose
- Increases completion rate of setup
- Improves adoption and retention

---

## Reflection

### 1. How do UX decisions impact adoption and retention?

- Good UX reduces friction → users complete setup faster
- Clear flows → users understand value → higher retention
- Poor UX leads to confusion → users abandon features

---

### 2. Common UX Mistake PMs Should Watch Out For

Overloading users with too many options without guidance.

---

### 3. Balancing UX vs Business Constraints

PMs must:

- Prioritise **core user value first**
- Limit scope for initial release (v1)
- Work with designers to simplify without losing functionality

---

## Key Takeaways

- UX directly impacts feature success and retention
- Simplicity is more important than flexibility for new users
- PMs should advocate for clarity and usability
- Structured onboarding flows significantly improve adoption
