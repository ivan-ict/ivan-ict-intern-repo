# Roles in an Agile Team

## 1. Research & Learning

### Key Roles in an Agile Team

Agile teams consist of several roles that collaborate closely to design, develop, test, and deliver valuable products. Each role contributes different expertise to ensure that work is completed efficiently and meets user needs.

---

### Developers

Developers are responsible for designing, building, and maintaining the product. Their tasks typically include writing code, implementing features, fixing bugs, and ensuring the technical quality of the system.

Developers work closely with other team members to understand product requirements and translate them into functional software. In Agile teams, developers are often cross-functional and collaborate with designers, QA engineers, and product managers throughout the development process.

---

### QA (Quality Assurance)

Quality Assurance (QA) specialists ensure that the product meets quality standards before it is delivered to users. Their responsibilities include testing features, identifying bugs, and verifying that the product functions as expected.

QA engineers may perform different types of testing, such as:

- functional testing
- regression testing
- usability testing
- performance testing

By identifying issues early, QA helps maintain product reliability and improve the user experience.

---

### Product Manager (PM)

The Product Manager is responsible for defining the product vision and ensuring that the development team builds features that deliver value to users.

Key responsibilities include:

- defining product requirements
- prioritizing the product backlog
- communicating the product vision
- coordinating with stakeholders
- ensuring alignment between business goals and development work

The Product Manager works closely with developers, designers, and other stakeholders to ensure the product evolves in the right direction.

---

### Agile Project Manager (Agile PM)

The Agile Project Manager (sometimes called Scrum Master in Scrum teams) focuses on facilitating the Agile process and helping the team operate effectively.

Responsibilities may include:

- organizing Agile ceremonies
- removing blockers that affect the team
- supporting team collaboration
- ensuring Agile practices are followed

The Agile PM helps maintain a smooth workflow and encourages continuous improvement within the team.

---

### Other Supporting Roles

In addition to core team members, several supporting roles contribute to the success of Agile projects.

**Stakeholders**

Stakeholders include business leaders, investors, or clients who influence product direction and priorities. They provide feedback and help ensure the product aligns with organizational goals.

**UX/UI Designers**

UX designers focus on improving user experience by designing intuitive interfaces and workflows. They conduct research, create wireframes, and ensure the product meets user needs.

**Customer Support**

Customer support teams interact directly with users and collect feedback about product issues or feature requests. This feedback can help guide future product improvements.

---

### Collaboration in Kanban vs Scrum

Agile teams collaborate differently depending on the framework they use.

**Scrum**

Scrum uses structured roles and ceremonies. Teams typically include a Product Owner, Scrum Master, and Development Team. Work is organized into fixed-length sprints, and ceremonies such as Sprint Planning, Daily Stand-ups, Sprint Reviews, and Retrospectives guide the workflow.

**Kanban**

Kanban focuses more on workflow visualization and continuous delivery rather than defined sprint cycles. Roles are generally less rigid, and responsibilities are more flexible. Work moves continuously through a Kanban board, and teams focus on managing workflow efficiency and limiting work in progress.

While Scrum emphasizes structured planning cycles, Kanban emphasizes **continuous flow and flexible collaboration**.

### Improving Teamwork With Developers

One way to improve teamwork with developers is by ensuring that tasks contain **clear descriptions, acceptance criteria, and expected outcomes** before they move into the development stage.

This can be achieved by:

- writing clearer issue descriptions
- providing detailed acceptance criteria
- discussing requirements before development begins

By improving clarity and communication, developers can work more efficiently and reduce misunderstandings, which ultimately improves the overall development workflow.
