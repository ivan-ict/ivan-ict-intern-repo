# Learning Goals for My Internship

## What Do I Hope to Learn From This Internship?

During this internship, I hope to gain a deeper understanding of how a real Agile product team operates in a professional environment. While I already have previous experience as a Product Owner, I want to observe how product decisions are made, how tasks are organized within a development workflow, and how teams collaborate effectively.

I am particularly interested in learning how a startup product team manages priorities, communicates across different roles, and continuously improves the product through iterative development.

In addition, I hope to better understand how productivity tools like Focus Bear are developed and improved based on user needs and feedback.

---

## What Skills Am I Most Excited to Develop?

The skills I am most excited to develop during this internship include:

**Product thinking**

I want to improve my ability to analyze user needs, evaluate product features, and think strategically about how a product creates value for its users.

**Agile workflow management**

Working with Agile tools such as Kanban boards, GitHub issues, and collaborative workflows will help me strengthen my ability to manage tasks and track product progress.

**Communication and collaboration**

Product roles require strong communication with developers, designers, and other stakeholders. I hope to improve my ability to clearly explain ideas, document requirements, and coordinate with different team members.

**Critical product analysis**

Through tasks such as competitive landscape analysis and feature evaluation, I want to improve my ability to analyze products critically and identify opportunities for improvement.

---

## What Do I Think I Can Contribute to the Team?

Although I am an intern, I believe I can contribute to the team in several ways.

First, my previous experience as an ICT Product Owner allows me to understand the basics of product management and Agile workflows. This background helps me quickly understand product discussions and task organization.

Second, I can contribute by organizing tasks clearly, documenting insights, and maintaining structured workflows through tools such as GitHub projects and Kanban boards.

Finally, I can contribute by providing thoughtful feedback and observations when analyzing product features, user experience, and competitor tools. A fresh perspective can sometimes help identify new ideas or improvements.

---

## One Area I Feel Less Confident In

One area where I feel less confident is **deep technical understanding of software development**.

While I understand product management concepts and Agile workflows, I would like to strengthen my understanding of technical implementation and development processes. This would allow me to communicate more effectively with developers and better understand technical constraints.

Improving this area will help me become a stronger bridge between product ideas and technical execution.

---

## What Success Looks Like for Me in This Internship

For me, success in this internship means that I finish the program with a stronger understanding of how real product teams work in practice.

Specifically, success would include:

- gaining practical experience working in an Agile environment
- improving my ability to organize and manage product-related tasks
- developing stronger communication and collaboration skills
- gaining deeper insight into how productivity products are designed and improved

If by the end of the internship I feel more confident in my ability to contribute to a product team and support product development decisions, I will consider the internship a successful learning experience.
